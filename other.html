Other Art

This page is for a whole other category of artist. Maybe their wor couldn't really be described as pottery or painting. Maybe their photographs bear paint on them as well. Still others here make films, or write, or do other things that can't be captured in a photograph. This is the place for other artists, and other patrons as well, who want to see something else.

Allie and Jake, the duo behind Anchored in Flight, have made it their mission to provide New Orleans with visionary art, apparel, and more. Anchored in Flight features a widespread of VisionLab art and apparel, along with fractal-design wooden boxes and carvings from local artist Linden Keal. Ceremonial feather fans by Allie and originals/prints by Jake are also exhibited.

You can find Anchored in Flight on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Andrew strives to make a name for himself in the art community through his work with wire. Some of the things he makes includes wearable wire sculpture, custom wire wraps and sculptures, and wire art.

Andrew's work can also be viewed on Facebook and Instagram.

Carla Moll graduated SLU with an Art Degree and is currently the Art Director for Mopar Collector's Guide Magazine. Some of the things she creates include steampunk lamps (made out of reclaimed wood and piping) and paintings (using the reverse side of reclaimed wooden windows).

Atmosphere33 can be found on their website.

Ben Dombey is a glass artist who has been working with glass for over a decade, even traveling the world to hone his skill. The glasses can be customized with different images and texts on the bottom. Each glass his individually handmade and inflated with 100% lungpower.

Ben's work can also be viewed on his website.

Kyle Herr started glassblowing in high school, focused on the medium at Alfred University, and now spends his time teaching and sharing his passion for glass with the public. His company makes decorative, functional, and sculptural handmade glass.

Black Dog Glass also has a website to view other works.

Brian Kissmal is a lifelong artist and educator. He is the author of Phonics Things - An ABC, Learn to Read Book for the 21st Century. Brian uses bold colors and textures in his oil paintings, and brings a musical rhythm to his poetry and writing.

Brian Kissman can also be found on his website.

Cailtlin Ezell Waugh explores the aesthetics of deca, vulnerability, and variation through treatment of metal, wood, glass, clay, and found objects. Her 'Enverre' line focuses on an ongoing development where glass plates are fired in a kiln and framed in a variety of ways.

Caitlin also runs Paraph, a restoration and design business, from her home studio in Uptown New Orleans.
website, Facebook, Instagram, Pinterest, and on Where Y'Art.

Cayla has gone from being a local shop girl to a middle school art teacher, and with some support, has taken her love of art to another level. Her work uses puns and birds in designs for paintings, prints, greeting cards, and thigs yet to come!

All cards are hand-illustrated and colored with watercolor.

Cayla's art can also be viewed on her website, Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Chandra is the product of a family tree littered with artists and musicians. Her desire to produce works of art has led to numerous awards, exhibitions, and commissions. Her current interest include: illustrative designs and mixed media works; and employing the use of drawing, painting, printmaking, collage, and recycled/reclaimed materials. Her works range from miniature canvasses to large murals, featuring original 2D designs in full color.

ChandarChandar also has a website, Etsy, Facebook, and Tumblr.

Chloe Rose is an emerging artist and costume designer from San Francisco. Some of her past work includes designs for the de Young Museum in San Francisco; a Frida Kahlo/Gaultier-inspired catwalk show, and "Project Nunway".

Gypsy Junk, her current work, uses layers of vintage flowers, lace, feathers, and appliques to create unique headpieces.

Chloe Rose's work can also be seen on Etsy, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Chris Cumbie makes his pieces by using the techniques of carving, painting, welding, illustration, whittling, and metalwork. He attempts to combine these different forms into one piece by recycling, recreating, refinishing, and redesigning all sorts of objects.

In addition to 3-D mixed media art, Chris also has a popular series of wood-carved characters called C.R.E.E.P.S.

Chris Cumbie's work can also be viewed on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Debo and Sam make unique, custom chainmail jewelry and other works of art. Some of the materials incorporated in their pieces include bones and skulls and recycled license plates. The things they'll create include custom signs, cuffs, bookmarks, keychains, and patches.

Debo and Sam's work can be viewed on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Dodeca Designs is a small group of friends that create handmade jewelry and crafts from local and exotic woods. Many pieces are made from scrapped or recycled woods. In addition to jewelry, the group also makes plugs, magic wine holders, business card holders, platters, turned bowls, and other wooden crafts.

Dodeca Designs can be found on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Dominic Sgro works to provide a broad range of interpretations, most of them being in the form of sketches, paints, and digitography. Amongst the mediums used include acrylic, watercolor, oil, pencil, and pen/ink. Canvas, linen, and Arches and Japonn paper provide blank slates. Later pieces of concept art, like matte paintings or numbered giclees, are produced as required.

Sgro has been exhibited at John Slade Eli House, Whitney Museuem of Art Stamford, and Uncommon Gallery. Past clients include WACHOVIA Bank, Brews & News, and Palm Beach Business Consultants.

Dominic's work can also be found on this website.

Drew Cooke is known for making vibrant photo collages and 3D paintings of pineapples. His works have been shown at galleries in New York City, New Orleans, and Oklahoma City. His pineapple works range in price from $80-$250; prints of his collages are available for $20.

Drew's work is also available on his website, Twitter, and Instagram.

Erato has been a visual artist since childhood, drawn to surrealism and spiritual topics. She can manifest a physical realization from both her own visions and from collaboration with others. Erato is mainly a painter, but also works in sculpture, jewelry, leatherwork, body painting, and henna.

Erato can also be found on Facebook.

Goods that Matter is committed to creating eco-friendly products in the U.S. that give back to the world’s most pressing social and environmental needs. To date, the company has donated $28,210 to wetland restoration, oil spill cleanup, disaster relief, education, and literacy groups.

Find Goods that Matter on their website, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Max Travers is a graphic designer and illustrator, whose latest project turns produce into characters with distinct style and personality. There's a Goofy Froot for everyone!

Max also works on several other comic book projects, along with his freelance work. His other works can be seen on Facebook.

Harley London products are handcrafted in a small workshop in New Orleans by a designer named Charlotte. Her concept was to create an eco-friendly, wood-centered brand. The concept has since grown to produce such products as cufflinks, lapel pins, pocket squares, coasters, postcards, bookmarks, and keychains.

Harley Londo can also be found on their website.

Hugh lives and works in New Orleans. He started making hand-carved drinking horns in 2015; the process involves carving images and texts on buffalo horns and lining then with beeswax to make them safe to drink out of.

Dail Chambers is a mother and community artist. Her work focuses on the migration of American black women and creatives through ethnographic, genealogical, and biographical research. Her art captures personal and political progress through sculpture, installation, and creative writing.

Dail's artwork can be viewed on her website.

Jeffrey Miller is equal parts education, natural talent, and life experience. His expertise is in installation art and assemblage, though he's also open to the variety of media yet to be explored. His current work is inspired by fireworks, especially what a little piece would look like under a microscope.

Jeffrey's work can also be seen on his website.

Jenna creates one-of-a-kind feather fascinators, meant to glorify and adorn the woman weearing it. Her work ranges from small and delicate to over-the-top and outrageous. Her prices range from $40-$95. She's very open to custom work.

Jenna's fascinators can be found on her website, Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Born in Ohio and reborn in New Orleans, Jess Leigh was moved to created raw stone and repurposed jewelry after moving to New Orleans in 2009. Each piece is independently designed and locally handmade. Repurposing vintage jewelry is just one of the ways the unique nature of the line stays strong. From fun costume pieces to lovely creations with semi-precious stones, there is something for every girl.

Jess Leigh's jewelry can also be found on her website, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Lorna Williams offers a line of artistic and unique writing utensils. Each handcrafted pen and pencil is one-of-a-kind in design and created to assist in the display and documentation of its user's character, visual creativity, and declarations.

Lorna Williams can be found on Facebook.

Working from antique imagery an her own photography, Loveday Funck plays with surrealistic themes and fairy tales, with pop culture references and historical sources in the mix. Her work explores the spiritual place where whimsy meets the unknown.

Loveday Funck's work can be viewed on her website.

Seonmi J. Richard, who is originally from South Korea, crafts flower-shaped lamps from Hanji. The light that passes through Hanjo creates a warm and cozy atmosphere, highlighted by the unique and beautiful textures inside.

Seonmi J. Richard can be found on Instagram.
