Mixed Media & Art Objects

Sometimes, art doesn't just fit into one single box. It can contain so many things from so many different sources, or it may be the first of its kind. This page is for the artists who either couldn't stick to one thing or created something totally new. They seek to appeal to someone who doesn't quite see things traditionally as well.

Allie and Jake, the duo behind Anchored in Flight, have made it their mission to provide New Orleans with visionary art, apparel, and more. Anchored in Flight features a widespread of VisionLab art and apparel, along with fractal-design wooden boxes and carvings from local artist Linden Keal. Ceremonial feather fans by Allie and originals/prints by Jake are also exhibited.

You can find Anchored in Flight on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Veronica's work involves sculpture, photography, printmaking, and painting. Many of her images are derived from photos taken in Mexico, New York, and New Orleans. Heather's artwork involves using a reverse painting process called Pareidolia Matrixing, where the paint is removed to leave behind a stained canvas. The resulting artwork is very abstract, almost like a Rorschach Ink Blot, where the mind is left to see shapes where there are none.

Items available include original paintings, limited replica postcards and prints, handpainted magnets, and sculptural pieces.

Heathergreyart can be found on her website, Etsy, and Facebook.

Matt Tumlinson is a self-taught artist raised in rural Texas. He is credited with developing the Brass Canvas, which utilizes spent pistol casings to create "canvas" for his paintings. Each cavas is made up of several hundred casings affixed to a backing, which can then be used with both oil and acrylic paints.

Tumlinson's work has been included in fine art galleries from Santa Fe to Nantucket Island.

Matt Tumlinson's other works can be found on his website.
