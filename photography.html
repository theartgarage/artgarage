Photography

In an age where we all carry cameras in our pockets, photography may seem to be something anyone can do. While anyone can take a picture, the photographers below have captured the fleeting beauty of a city moment - or perhaps something deeper than that. The photographs at the Garage promise moments of emotion, glimpses of angles previously unseen, by those that wished to find them.

Atre Jeffrey has been a professional photographer since 1997, and has had his work featured in multiple publications around the world. He's also helped to co-found the St. Claude Arts District, and he also owns a top-rated tour company.

His work includes photographic prints applied to found wood and other objects, along with hand-painted folk art signs.

Arte Jeffrey also has a website.

Big Al has been photographing southern Louisiana since moving to New Orleans in 2008. His work in street photography also draws attention to what he calls the '100-year expiration date': New Orleans may vanish in our lifetimes due to rising sea levels. His goal in life is to capture everything before it's gone.

Big Al the Camera Man also has a website and can be found on Instagram.

Blake Broadhead is a local artist with a mixed-media take on culture.

Be sure to check out Blake Broadhead's website!

Chrystal Lea Nause is a fine art photographer specializing in traditional darkroom processes. She earned her Bachelor of Arts (photography, 2005) and Master of Arts (physical anthropology, 2010) from Southern Illinois University Carbondale. Her primary interests are travel photography, narrative tableau, and classic portraiture. Her work has been exhibited internationally.

In addition to photography, she also operate two other businesses: Nuttier Than A Squirrel and BOMB BOMBS.

You can find Chrystal Lea Nause on her website, Etsy, Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Corrie Boudreaux is a documentary photographer bases in New Orleans. Her work focuses on urban and natural landscapes, social violence, and street photography. She has taught courses at Tulane University and has had her work exhibited in Ciudad Juarez, Mexico; and San Juan, Puerto Rico. Corrie also volunteers with Pablove Shutterbugs and the My New Orleans Photo Project.

Her work can also be found on her website, Facebook, Instagram, and her website.

David Joshua Jennings, whose photography began as an outgrowth of his nonfictional writing, explores such themes as globalization<, alienation, desire, and cultural dissolution.

His photographs can be found on his website and Instagram.

Gavin Johnson's love for photography stems from capturing the culture and ambiance of southern living. During his city explorations, he seeks ot the characteristics of the city that lends themselves well to still photography, whether they be the food or an abandoned building decaying years after Katrina passed through.

Gavin Johnson's work can also be found on his website, Facebook, and Instagram.

"Jack" Johnson, U.S. Army veteran, describes his work as simply looking for beauty, whether made by nature or by man. It was only recently that he's taken what he's learned in sales and tourism and blended it with his photography. His work has been seen from many around the world, who he'll happily call his friends.

"Jack" Johnson can be found on Facebook.

Jean Manale's goal is to capture the feel of the city, its culture, and its people. Her work has been published in Gambit Weekly and New Orleans Magazine. Her photographs have also been displayed at the Contemporary Arts Center, NOMA, Lemieux Gallery, and other venues citywide. She has been doing art markets for more than 12 years, including the former Bywater Art Market, the Arts Market of New Orleans, and the Frenchmen Art Market.

Joseph Walton is a New Orleans-based photographer whose most recent works can be seen in Black and White Magazine and the Multimedia Art Museum in Moscow. His focus is on street photography from various cities around the world.

Nicolas Pinto is an Italian-American photographer, whose view of people and places is influenced by his time in the Army. In addition to having his photographs displayed in galleries nationwide, he also teaches for the Leica Akademie.

Nicolas Pinto also has his own website and Instagram.

River Shepard is the work of Jennifer Brunjes, a multimedia visual artist. She produces cyanotypes--classic photographs and illustrations making up a body of work that illicits a feeling of connection to your surroundings.

River Shepard can be found on Instagram.

Scott Kerschner uses a computer and a camera to 'paint digitally', as he describes it. His interest in the history and evolution of art has an influence on what his drawn into his art.

The Hidden South is an ongoing project that documents the stories of those often overlooked by society. The project was started in September 2014 by Brent Walker.

In 2015, followers of the project funded its first book, The Hidden South - Come Home . This enabled Brent to take a trip around the southeast and publish the book in 2016.

Brent currently travels the South, collecting portraits and stories. You can see his work on his website, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Zee Amer came from Paris as a fashion photographer. She does both photography and painting. Her work focuses on French Quarter houses, concepts, people, fashion, and nudes.

Zee Amer's work can be found on her website.
