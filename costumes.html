Costumes & Masks

In New Orleans, costuming is an important part of our culture. Just as we'll find any excuse to have a party, there are plenty of people who will jump at the chance to go all out with a costume. From elaborate works worthy of contest wins to your next Mardi Gras mask, our featured designers have got you covered!

Costuming is a tradition in New Orleans, and the creator of Halfshell Productions takes pride in making beautiful pieces for the city's festivities. Halfshell Productions uses materials such as feathers, appliques, oyster shells, Spanish moss, and even alligator heads to make her headdresses.

Halfshell Productions is an active participator in art markets across the Gulf Coast region, and has shipped her works across the nation and beyond. You can find more on this artist on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

NOTE: Every headdress is custom made, so there may be slight variations in feathers. Rush orders for the holidays can be accommodated. Please message her for information about custom orders.

Hellbent creates unique, hand-molded leather leather costume masks for every Mardi Gras. You can find leather and non-leather holster bags year-round, along with bags for your hips, thighs, shoulders, and boots.

Other accessories include: recycled bike tube belts, fashion harnesses, wrist wallets, utility belts, armor, cuffs, and one-of-a-kind costume pieces.

Find Hellbent on their website, Etsy, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Prancing Peacock Designs is your source for handmade, creative fascinators and custom millenery. Whimsical fascinators, headdresses, crowns, and headpieces of all sorts can be found to suit any occasion. Each look combines a love for the colorful world we live in and a playful take on everyday life.

Lila Zucker and her creations can be found on her website, Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Robot Leather crafts hand-tooled leather masks for decoration and celebration. Their skills have provided the company with international exposure and a worldwide customer base.

Robot Leather can be found on Instagram.

Satori Nightshade is a multimedia artist, performer, and director. They're focused on psychshamanic, mythological, magical, and surreal works. Some featured pieces include handmade neoprene masks and phantom-limb clay jewelry.
