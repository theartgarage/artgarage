Pottery

From the functional to the fun, pottery can be used to create almost anything one can think of sculpting. The artists featured below are proud to bring you wares ranging from practical bowls and jugs to decorative bird sculptures.

Cailtlin Ezell Waugh explores the aesthetics of deca, vulnerability, and variation through treatment of metal, wood, glass, clay, and found objects. Her 'Enverre' line focuses on an ongoing development where glass plates are fired in a kiln and framed in a variety of ways.

Caitlin also runs Paraph, a restoration and design business, from her home studio in Uptown New Orleans.

Goods that Matter is committed to creating eco-friendly products in the U.S. that give back to the world’s most pressing social and environmental needs. To date, the company has donated $28,210 to wetland restoration, oil spill cleanup, disaster relief, education, and literacy groups.

Their launch product, BirdProject Soap, originally raised funds for cleanup and restoration after the 2010 BP Oil Spill. Each black bird-shaped soap contains a white ceramic bird, handmade from Louisiana Clay, which remains as a keepsake once the outer soap has washed away. The soap is shaped to be cradled in your hand and is a powerful representation of all creatures affected by the BP Deepwater Horizon Oil Spill.

Find Goods that Matter on their website, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Olberto Liza started his work in art at the age of 16, following a family tradition. He makes various objects out of clay. Liza is a former member of the Association of Cuban Artists.

Pottery by Osa crafts functonal, handmade terra cotta pottery for everyday use. Te creatr tries to tie their craft to social justice causes whenever possible.

All items are food, microwave, and dishwasher safe.

The creator of Rabbit Starr has taken multiple forms of art and uses them to create creepy, colorful dolls. With her skills as a painter, seamstress, and sculptor, she makes the dolls with sculpy, acrylic paint, and eccentric fabrics.

Rabbit Starr's work can be seen on Facebook and Instagram.

Ramiro Diaz, creator and lead designer for Ramiro Diaz Art, has a degree in Art Restoration from ISA Higher Institute of Art in Havana, Cuba. His work has been in painting, sculpting, welding, and woodcarving; but his true passion lies in ceramics. Several of his pieces are on display in the International Ceramics Art Museum in Havana.

All products are unique, hand-sculpted, hand-painted pieces. THe catalog features a variety of products. Commissions and special orders are welcome.

Ramiro Diaz Art can be found on Facebook and Instagram.

Brian Stanley uses local history, tradition, and material to create unique and one-of-a-kind pieces for your home, yard, and office. A wide variety of beautiful and functional art pieces are made using concrete and recycled material, from garden gnomes to food-safe bowls.

Stanley's work can be viewed on his website, Etsy, and Facebook.

Vixon Sullivan is a designer who works in various mediums. His largest body work consists of handmade porcelain collectables, such as jewelry, functional pottery, and ceramic sculptures.

View more of Vixon Sullivan's work on his website, Facebook, and Instagram
