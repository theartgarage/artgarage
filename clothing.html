Clothing, Textiles, & Accessories

Some designers want to start trends. Others end up putting a whole new twist on something familiar. The artists in this category have made all sorts of items for anyone's wardrobe. Whether you want a bold statement or a subtle yet defined piece, there's something for everyone here.

Beyond the Garage is the collaboration of a creative couple who simply enjoy making things. Clay Achee and Kirstin Laire have worked together in diverse media. Their main attraction are hand-made "Muppet"-style puppets; Monster Hats are also sold.

Find Beyond the Garage on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Bonga-Chop Designs uses recycled fabrics to create original clothing and accessories. The clothing is sold off the rack, but custom orders are also taken.

Kids' dresses and shirts sell for around $65. Adult clothing ranges from $100-$250 a piece. Bags and wallets range from $20-$125.

The fabrics are sourced from local thrift stores and are 100% recycled.

Look for Bonga-Chop Designs on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Charles Anderson paints abstract blazers that, to him, are a true New Orleans experience. The blazers are like walking paintings, and prove that fashion allows for fine art to be in the sight of the people.

Charles's art has been covered by ABC News, Fox 8 News, The Times-Picayune, The Trumpet, and BreakThru Magazine. He has also organized hundred of marches, vigils, art shows, and classes in the most violent parts of New Orleans.

Charles's work can be found on Etsy and Instagram.

Chloe Rose is an emerging artist and costume designer from San Francisco. Some of her past work includes designs for the de Young Museum in San Francisco; a Frida Kahlo/Gaultier-inspired catwalk show, and "Project Nunway".

Gypsy Junk, her current work, uses layers of vintage flowers, lace, feathers, and appliques to create unique headpieces.

Chloe Rose's work can also be seen on Etsy, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Chubbs Made Leather Goods by Michael James has a variety of handmade leather goods, including: wallets, belts, guitar straps, bags, knife sheaths, and more. The New Orleans-based leather wizard behind every hand-done piece is also open to custom work.

Michael James's work can also be viewed on Instagram.

Designs by Masue aspires to create memorable, distinct prints. Hand-drawn original textiles are silkscreened onto linens of all shapes and sizes.

Other works by Designs by Masue can be found on her website, Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Edward Wycliff started to sew back when the Peace Corps sent him to Lesotho. Now, he is a self-trained fashion designer specializing in the use of hand-crank sewing machines. His work involves the communities he befriended in Lesotho, as he trains communites to produce accessories from traditional fabric while developing a new approach of poverty reuction and identity empowerment.

Edward Wycliff's works can be seen on his website, Etsy, and Facebook.

Costuming is a tradition in New Orleans, and the creator of Halfshell Productions takes pride in making beautiful pieces for the city's festivities. Halfshell Productions uses materials such as feathers, appliques, oyster shells, Spanish moss, and even alligator heads to make her headdresses.

Halfshell Productions is an active participator in art markets across the Gulf Coast region, and has shipped her works across the nation and beyond. You can find more on this artist on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

NOTE: Every headdress is custom made, so there may be slight variations in feathers. Rush orders for the holidays can be accommodated. Please message her for information about custom orders.

Mimi is a spirited New Orleans native with a knack for ecstatic dance, gypsy traveling, and making hats. All hats are 100% handmade from premium felt and straw. Speciality hats are made from mixed media such as vinyl records. All sizes are welcome for custom order.

Mimi Holaday's work can also be viewed on her website, Facebook, and Instagram.

Hellbent creates unique, hand-molded leather leather costume masks for every Mardi Gras. You can find leather and non-leather holster bags year-round, along with bags for your hips, thighs, shoulders, and boots.

Other accessories include: recycled bike tube belts, fashion harnesses, wrist wallets, utility belts, armor, cuffs, and one-of-a-kind costume pieces.

Find Hellbent on their website, Etsy, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

The creative mind behind Imaginess sculpts fabric, fibers, and leather into artforms that can be exhibited or worn. With costuming experience in major film productions (including Man of Steel, X-Men: The Last Stand, and Dawn of the Planet of the Apes) and television (Once Upon a Time and Odysseus, among others), she hopes to translate ideas into tangible, wearable art.

The work of Imaginess can be found on Etsy, Facebook, and Instagram.

Phillip Whitmore is a New Orleans artist and art teacher. Printmaking is his current concentration; he draws and designs all the shirts, aprons, prints, soft art, and other items for Inexplicable Confetti. His wife sews the aprons.

Inexplicable Confetti can be found on website, Etsy, Facebook, and Twitter.

Akeem Agbelekale learned his trade at an early age from generations of Nigerian cloth artisans, and currently works on reverse applique and Batik Wearable Arts. Batik is a technique of wax-resist dyeing applied to cloth by drawing dots and lines with the resists, before removing the wax and repeating.

Kelsey Campion is a fine art photographer and designer, who seeks to bring simplicity and beauty into both her photography and design. In addition to photography, she also makes chic headwraps and turbans, inspired by Italian design.

Her clothing designs can be found under Clothing/Textiles/Accesories.

Kelsey Campion can be found on her website, Etsy, Facebook, and on Instagram.

Kinfolk Collective is the work of Janna Hart, a New Orleans native and fashion designer. Her company is a sock brand with a mission: fr every pair sold, a pair gets donated to local shelters in the city. The socks are Louisiana-themed, with embroidered streetcar logos.

Kinfolk Collective can also be found on their website, Facebook, and Instagram.

Aubrey Fullerton is a modern-day renaissance woman and holder of the following titles: leathermaker, photographer, makeup artist, stylist, lumberjack, handyman, swing dancer, pig lover, motorcyclist, and mother. Some of the leather goods made include backpacks, purses, jewelry, keychains, harp rolls, and other odds and ends.
