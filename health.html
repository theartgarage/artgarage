Home, Health, & Beauty

Home is an important part of everyone's life It's where the heart is at, and to many, there's no where else like it. Everyone always wants the best for their health, especially in the current wave of mass-produced mania. The artists below hope that their works will help improve your home, health, or beauty in some way. Whether you need something allergen-free, sustainably made, or just crafted by hand, these artists have your best interests in mind.

Allie's Natural Hair Community handcrafts hair products using natural, organic, and Ayurvedic ingredients. No matter your ethnicity, hair type, or texture, Allie hopes that her products can suit your needs.

All products are multipurpose.

Find Allie's Natural Hair Community on her website.

Coconola Skincare specializes in natural, handmade skin care.

No chemicals, no preservative, no nonsense. It's health food for the skin!

Find Coconola Skincare on their website, Etsy, and Facebook.

Diosa de Barro Earthenware produces functional wares out of clay, such as cups, mugs, bowls, and lanterns. The pieces are inspired by a combination of natural landscapes, gems & minerals, ornate henna, and a love for all things spooky.

You can find Diosa de Barro on Instagram.

Essentially Nola creates artisan bath, body & skin care products boosted with the healing powers of essential oils.

Their products are made in small batches to ensure quality, with 100% organic bases. The ingredients sourced are 100% food grade and do not contain gluten, parabens, or GMOs.

Please note that raw coconut oil is used in most products.

You can also find Essentially NOLA on their website, Facebook, and on Instagram.

Feminist Flames creates deliciously scented candles to soothe the wronged women of this world. Their goal is to create a soothing scent to get women through every curve ball the patriarchy throws at them. Their candles are made in tiny 8-candle batches on a home stove. Each batch's scent and color is personally tested before being lovingly poured and handled.

The company is seen as a way of healing our own wounds and helping other women support each other.

Feminist Flames can be found on Etsy, Facebook, and on Amazon.

Josh Wascome is a mixed-media artist and woodworker. His crafts include: bowls, Christmas ornaments, tables, candlestick holders, wine stoppers, salad paddles, platters, cheese and sushi trays, and even sinks.

All crafts are made from local wood.

Joshua Wascome can be found on Etsy and Facebook.

Fine quality wooden kitchen utensils, spoons, spatulas, toys and jewelry. These are some of the things crafted by Kanga in their Lancaster, PA, woodshop. All products are natural hardwood with food-safe oil/wax finishes.

Kanga's work can also be viewed on Etsy and Facebook.

The duo behind Old SOuls may be well-traveled, but New Orleans is always their home. With their work best described as "farm punk", some of the home decor items made include a lamp made from car parts; a candelabra crafted from a fireplace tool stand and a cam shaft; and side tables made from suitcases.

In addition, a variety of jewelry is also available.

Old Souls can be found on Etsy, Facebook, and Pinterest.

Seonmi J. Richard, who is originally from South Korea, crafts flower-shaped lamps from Hanji. This traditional, handmade paper is made of the inner bark of the mulberry tree; and grows well on the rocky mountainsides of Korea. The light that passes through Hanji creates a warm and cozy atmosphere, highlighted by the unique and beautiful textures inside.

Seonmi J. Richard can be found on Instagram.

The London Clayworks is run by Karin and Rebecca, who both teach at the studio as well. WOrking mostly on the wheel, the two make one-of-a-kind pieces from porcelain and stoneware clay. Their work is both functional and decorative, and includes kitchenware, home decor, and jewelry. The pieces range in choices and price points for any budget.

The London Clay Works can also be found on their website, Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest.

Trey Lewis makes New Orleans-themed prayer candles to pay homage to the New Orleanians who have shaped the culture of the city, and to celebrate them in a religious saint-like manner. Among those in the lineup include St. Lil' Wayne, St. Doctor John, St. Paul Prudhomme, and Saint Mr. Okra.

Each candle is one of a kind and handmade.

Trey Lewis's work can be found on Etsy.
